Waiting For Recovery

Waiting For Recovery

Apr 25, 2022 | 2 min | Apoorva Khandelwal

The pandemic has brought many attacks and inconveniences for everybody –including young adults – such as motivational slumps, burnout, and familial disconnect.

According to UNICEF, one in two adolescents don’t feel motivated to commit to activities they once enjoyed. When the pandemic started, stores, sports, and schools shut down, confining all to their homes. For athletes, their sports might have brought them confidence and bonds, but with everything shutting down, practicing with the team was a challenge. When these sports reopened again, many athletes that didn’t practice during the lockdown fell behind and lost their motivation to continue their sport. Additionally, because a large chunk of activities, like volunteering and club meets, took place at school, the lockdown forced many to quit and lose out on their time spent together.

Another problem with the shutdown was the intense burnout brought upon students. Taking advantage of the online school year, teens started working harder to ensure their grades.. Even if they wore comfortable clothes and were surrounded by a familiar environment, countless teens became uncomfortable because of how strenuous it was to work for several hours on end. For me, school lasted six hours straight on a computer. I never even took breaks because during the passing period, I would frantically be searching for the next class’ Zoom link. Then, at lunch, I would watchNetflix to entertain myself. My eyes never got a break, and I was tired nearly everyday of online school.

For most, a neutral surrounding to have during the lockdown was family. According to Kids’ Minds Matter, families have reported by getting their bonds to grow closer and stronger who were able to cooperate with the tens and their lives. However, certain families grew apart: young adults with abusive parents stressed out and itched to get out of the house.

A challenge for adolescents now would be getting back on track and trying to find themselves after getting out of lockdown (depending on the state). After a harsh year of being stuck in the house and losing ourselves, it is time to wait,recover, and hope for the best.
